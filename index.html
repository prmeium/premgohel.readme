baithak

ongoing

baithak started as a simple idea:

what if we built a space where builders, creators, and operators actually showed up — not to flex, but to build?

no panels for the sake of panels.
no networking that feels forced.
just rooms with people who care about shipping things.

it turned into meetups, workshops, cohorts, side projects, and long conversations that didn't end when the event did.

building baithak taught me something important:
community isn't about scale first — it's about signal.

get the room right, and everything compounds.

content & community

i don't separate content and community.
they're the same thing to me.

good content attracts the right people.
good people create better content.
repeat.

that loop is what i'm obsessed with.

whether it's a reel, a website, an event, or a cohort — i think in terms of:

does this feel honest?
does this feel intentional?
would i show up to this if my name wasn't on it?

if the answer is no, we rework it.
